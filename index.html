Prof. Dr. Ing. Rozenn DAHYOT

Professor of Computer Science

I am Professor of Computer at Maynooth University in Ireland since April 2021. I was previously Associate Professor in Statistics in the School of Computer Science and Statistics in Trinity College Dublin Ireland. I am a member of the ADAPT research Centre for Digital Content Technology that focuses on developing next generation digital technologies and regroups researchers accross several leading Irish Higher Education Institutions. I lead the effort for organising the European Signal Processing Conference EUSIPCO2021 in Dublin in 2021 (August 23-27th 2021).

My research interests are in between the traditional fields of Electronic and electrical engineering (digital signal processing), Computer Science (computer vision & graphics), artificial intelligence (data mining, machine learning) and Mathematics (Statistics, Information theory, decision theory, functional analysis).

Road Scene Analysis

Research & Commercialisation

We propose to geolocate objects (e.g. poles, road signs,trees) using AI for analysing street view imagery. Many applications such as autonomous navigation, urban planning and asset monitoring, rely on the availability of accurate geolocations. We are now investigating commercial opportunities (Enterprise Ireland commercialisation fund).

Latest publication: Remote Sensing (2018)


  • aimapit.com

Climate change

Research

Climate change is affecting people and their way of life including transportation of goods and people. Our work (2019) proposes to analyse satellite imagery in combination with street view imagery posted on social media during flooding events, for assessing the passability of roads. More recently (2020), we propose to infer 3d shape of roofs from aerial imagery (e.g. for measuring solar energy potentials).

Latest publications: Remote Sensing (2020)
Signal Processing: Image Communication (2019)

Artificial Intelligence

Research

Deep Learning (or Machine Learning with Neural Networks) is a very popular approach for designing data-driven artificial intelligence (AI). It is used in many of our applications for object detection, image enhancement, data synthesis etc.

Latest publications: IEEE ICASSP (2021), ACM IoT (2020)
